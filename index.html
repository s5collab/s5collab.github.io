Southern Stellar Stream Spectroscopic Survey

About S⁵

Mapping stellar streams of the Milky Way with the Anglo-Australian Telescope

S⁵ is a spectroscopic survey began in 2018 with the 2dF/AAOmega spectrograph on the Anglo-Australian Teelscope. The aim is to map the kinematics and chemistry of stellar streams in the Southern Hemisphere. The initial focus has been on recently identified streams within the footprint of the Dark Energy Survey (DES), with the eventual goal of surveying the entire southern sky.

The streams represent material tidally striped from dwarf galaxies and globular clusters and hence are essential dynamical probes of the dark matter distribution of the Milky Way, as well as providing a detailed snapshot of its accretion history.

Observing with the 3.9-m Anglo-Australian Telescope's 2-degree-Field (2dF) fibre positioner and AAOmega spectrograph, and combining the precise photometry of DES DR1 with the superb proper motions from Gaia DR2, allows us to conduct an efficient spectroscopic survey to map these DES streams. In addition to the stream survey, we use spare fibres to undertake a Milky Way halo survey and a low-redshift galaxy survey.

S⁵-Streams

The main survey. The goal is to measure radial velocities and metallicities of stream members. So far S⁵ has mapped 9 DES streams and 3 streams outside of DES; the former are the first spectroscopic observations of these recently discovered streams.

S⁵-Halo

We are able to use some spare fibres for a Milky Way halo survey. This has the aim of observing interesting objects in the halo such as hyper-velocity stars, extremely metal-poor stars, RR Lyraes, and white dwarfs.

(Credit: ESO/L. Calçada)

S⁵-LowZ

Nearby faint galaxies are difficult to distinguish from the far more numerous background galaxy population via photometry alone. The goal of including low-redshift (low-z) galaxy targets in S⁵ is to increase the number of spectroscopically confirmed low-z galaxies in order to better train photometric selection algorithms, and help build a statistic sample of very low-z galaxies.

(Credit: SDSS)

S⁵ Membership

The S⁵ Collaboration consists of roughly 30 members spread across many institutions.

S⁵ is a collaboration between the members DES Milky Way Working Group and a group of Australian astronomers.

If you are interested in getting involved in S⁵, please contact Ting Li or other members in the S⁵ leadership team.
